Sources & methodology

How the traffic light works

Zoomed out (regional view): each bubble is a cluster of stations. Its colour compares the area's average price with the national average of each station's own country — green ≤ −1.5%, red ≥ +1.5%, amber in between. This keeps colours meaningful across borders.

Zoomed in (station view): colours re-rank against the stations currently on screen — cheapest third green, middle third amber, priciest third red — so you can always spot the best stop on a route.

How to read each price — trust tiers

  • 🟢 Government · per station — official national price feeds, refreshed every run. Highest trust; use these to decide where to fuel.
  • 🔵 Official national average — one figure per country (EU Weekly Oil Bulletin), drawn as a dashed bubble at the country centre. Official but not per-station — indicative for planning only.
  • 🟡 Community — Open Charge Map (EV). Charger location & power are reliable; price is community-supplied and often missing.
  • ⚪ Location only — grey dot, no price (OpenStreetMap), for diesel in countries with no public price feed. Shows where to fuel, not the price.

Methodology & updates

Everything refreshes automatically every day at 06:00 UTC (plus any manual run). If an official feed is down on refresh day (Spain’s server, for example, rejects connections some days), the last good data is kept and marked, and the next run replaces it — the map never fabricates a price. UK prices convert £→€ at the daily ECB rate. EV prices are sanity-checked (values above €1.20/kWh are treated as non-euro or per-session tariffs and dropped). OpenStreetMap locations are cached in the repository, so a country’s stations never disappear if OSM rate-limits a run. National averages marked ≈ are the official EU weekly bulletin figures (or pinned indicative values for Norway and Switzerland), not station-level prices.

EV charging — all countries (community data)

Where it comes from: ⚡ Open Charge Map (openchargemap.org), the largest open registry of charge points, covering all 21 countries in this tool. For every charger it gives location, power rating (kW), operator, and connector type (AC / DC / HPC).

Limitations: Most chargers have no public price because EV tariffs depend on the driver’s charging plan or roaming contract. No official live EV-price feed exists anywhere in Europe, so per-kWh price cannot be guaranteed and is shown only where an operator publishes it. Open Charge Map is community-maintained, so a few entries may be out of date. Location and power are reliable — that is what the Van/Truck/HPC power filter at the top uses.

Refresh: pulled fresh every day at 06:00 UTC together with the rest of the data, plus any time the pipeline is run manually.

Updates

Refreshes automatically every day at 06:00 UTC via an automated pipeline; you can also trigger it manually. No sample or estimated station data is ever shown — if a country or fuel has no trusted source, it stays empty.
