Sources & methodology

How the traffic light works

Zoomed out (regional view): each bubble is a cluster of stations. Its colour compares the area's average price with the national average of each station's own country — green ≤ −1.5%, red ≥ +1.5%, amber in between. This keeps colours meaningful across borders.

Zoomed in (station view): colours re-rank against the stations currently on screen — cheapest third green, middle third amber, priciest third red — so you can always spot the best stop on a route.

  • 📋 Manual override — data/manual/national_averages.csv (country,fuel,eur) replaces any average by hand

Current status

This tool shows live data from official sources: government fuel-price feeds for Spain, France, Italy, the UK and Austria (per-station Diesel and HVO), the European Commission Weekly Oil Bulletin for official national diesel averages where no station feed exists, and Open Charge Map for EV chargers. It refreshes automatically every Monday 06:00 UTC. No sample or estimated station data is shown.
